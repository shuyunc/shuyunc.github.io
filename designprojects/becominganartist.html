Enhancing the Visiting Experience for Museum Visitors

Chimera Art Museum
Role
UI Designer
Client
Chimera Art Museum
Duration
4 Weeks

Design an information kiosk and sketching app for a museum to showcase an artist's work and give visitors the ability to create artwork in the style of the artist

Challenge

The biggest challenge of this project is to think about how to make the experience flow organically. Because most of the time when people create art using an app or kiosk, it is very hard to focus on the current moment. I researched how to stay at the current moment, and the results turned out focusing one task at a time helps. Therefore, the idea of using a prompt by Oksana is a great way to help users focus on the task at one time. It also helps to relieve the anxiety of making mistakes. 


“I would love to experience how to create artwork like an artist!”

How might we design an information kiosk for visitors to learn about the artwork and create their own piece using that unique artist's art making process?                                         



Featured Artist

Nature-inspired, mix medium of watercolor and graphite,
calming and relaxing.

Oksana’s work has a nice spontaneity quality. Her works are mostly inspired by nature: Shoreline, mountain, sand. The mixed medium of washes of watercolor and graphite can be easily built in the workspace into the kiosk.


Began by brainstorming the concept map, I found the main concepts are spontaneity, freeing the intuition and under the moment.

Using the video of nature as an inspiration board, users can freely express themselves when looking at the beauty and listening to the sound of nature.

Give user the process overview ahead of time.

Then I thought about my site map that can make user’s experience more seamless. Referencing the sitemap, I sketched my concepts of the UI and added visual elements to make my concept more solid and understandable.

Making user stay at current.

Because most of the time when people create art using an app or kiosk, it can be hard to stay under the current moment. By focusing one task at a time can effectively relieve the anxiety of making mistakes during the art making process. Then I had this idea of using Oksana's voice as a prompt, at the beginning of each stage, as it is a great way to help users staying under the moment.

Insights

Creating a sense of afar from the current surrounding can bring randomness and spontaneity that people feel when they travel.
‍
Allowing user to choose their favorite spot to get inspired is like taking them to a different part of the world. There they can freely express themselves.

Reflection

When I was designing this project, it was at the middle of the pandemic. I understand how social distancing stopped people in a constrained space from getting inspired by the nature and art around us. I see it as a way to give user the freedom to choose wherever they want to go as a source of inspiration to express their emotions. Using simple shapes and colors, I hope this museum kiosk can make them feel the flow when creating their unique art piece.
