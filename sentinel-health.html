Helping Busy Clinicians Identifying Care Priority

Sentinel Healthcare
Role
UX Research & UI Design
Team
2 UX Consultants
Client
Sentinel Healthcare
Duration
4 Weeks

In this project, I helped my client to identify the major pain points in their clinician's dashboard by conducting several interviews and usability testings with different stakeholders within the company. The redesigned dashboard resolves the care priority problem and has a positive impact.

Sentinel is a start-up company that specializes in remote patient monitoring. They provide a platform for both their clinical customers and their internal care teams to record and update patients’ health information. After expanding its business, they found their current medical dashboard is creating friction for its users.

Sentinel’s Company Vision

“We founded Sentinel because we believe that doctors need access to better data to make the best possible clinical decisions for their patients. “                                            

Keeping Company’s Mission in our mind, we want to prioritize understanding clinical customers needs and pain points. Because they are not only Sentinel’s main source of income but also the ones that the company really trying to help.

Interface of current dashboard is too complicated.

To ensure our redesign fulfills both business and user needs, and understand how the business works. I decided to conduct usability testings


Our user

Through surveys and Interviews, we found that most of users share these common characteristics:

They manage a small to mid-size clinic, they have many patients to manage. They don’t want to spend time on organizing documents of their patients.They want to focus on spending the most energy on patients who needs them the most as well as providing a high quality care to everyone.

Patient communication is where user encounters friction.

These interviews helped me to draw a system map for the company. By mapping out the user's experience from the beginning to the end, it helps to clarify the major pain points and places for improvements. The user journey is built from clinical customer’s perspective, however my goal here is to understand how each element interacts with each other to work as a body. I want to make sure while improving clinical customer's experience, internal team’s workflow can also be improved.

Pain Points
  • Navigation is not actively used by most users
  • Clinical customers do not see all the filtering tabs or majority of platform
  • Users would like to see the alert feature
  • The dashboard is only helpful for some users
  • Screens too crowded with multiple navigation options
  • Patient information is very “busy” and requires too much scrolling
Insights

Improving Note Organization and Dashboard is a Win-Win
‍
After circling the pain points, we found that when if we tackle clinical customer’s pain points, by redesigning note organization, and patients’ data overview, we can improve everyone’s workflow. This is a big win-win.

Results
  • Navigation is not actively used by most users
  • Clinical customers do not see all the filtering tabs or majority of platform
  • Users would like to see the alert feature
  • The dashboard is only helpful for some users
  • Screens too crowded with various navigation options
  • Patient information is very “busy” and requires too much scrolling
Reflection

Be proactive of the reason in every design decision using facts and data.
‍
We got very positive feedback with users including the Sentinel Care team, Managerial, and tech support team. I am glad that we insisted on going through all the design phases before making the final prototype. Something I learned from this experience is that even if it is important to understand needs from different perspectives, when time is limited, prioritizing the features which can bring the most value to users' need and the business goal.
